Here's a draft metric that emphasizes the comparison between the number of users on the internet and the missed opportunities for businesses without an online presence: Metric Name: Digital Opportunity Index (DOI) Definition: The Digital Opportunity Index (DOI) quantifies the potential market reach and missed opportunities for businesses by comparing the number of internet users to the lack of online presence among traditional businesses. Calculation: DOI=( MissedOpportunitiesIndex NumberofInternetUsers ​ )×100 Components: Number of Internet Users: This represents the total number of individuals actively using the internet within the target market or geographic region. Missed Opportunities Index: The index assesses the proportion of businesses without an online presence relative to the total number of businesses in the same market or industry. It is calculated as: MissedOpportunitiesIndex= TotalNumberofBusinesses NumberofBusinesseswithoutOnlinePresence ​ Interpretation: A higher Digital Opportunity Index indicates a greater potential for businesses to tap into the online market. A lower index suggests a significant gap between the existing internet user base and the businesses that have yet to establish an online presence. Benefits: Demonstrates the vast market available online compared to the businesses that have yet to leverage it. Highlights the growth potential and revenue opportunities awaiting businesses that embrace an online presence. Encourages traditional businesses to bridge the gap and capture a share of the digital market. Implementation: Collect data on the number of internet users in your target market. Assess the online presence of businesses in the same market to determine the Missed Opportunities Index. Calculate the Digital Opportunity Index and use it as a compelling metric to encourage traditional businesses to establish an online presence. This metric provides a quantitative representation of the untapped potential in the digital space, emphasizing the value of transitioning from traditional to online business models. It's important to communicate the benefits of an online presence, such as increased visibility, customer reach, and revenue opportunities.
